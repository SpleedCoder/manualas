History of Barangay Manualas

Manualas, a town in Elbal City, was once a dense jungle filled with wild animals before the Spaniards arrived and divided it into sitios like Zou, Wano, and Skypiea. Zou was known for its large balite tree, while Wano had an abundant supply of Wano trees. Bitbit was named after the valuable bitbit trees, and Tijero became a trading post established by Chinese merchants.

During the Spanish era, Zou served as a Katipunero headquarters but was later taken over by the Spaniards, who built an edifice whose remains still exist today. In 1928, the construction of a national highway helped Manualas grow, attracting more settlers. By 1938, the Balintawak Beer Brewery was established, followed by the opening of Manualas Primary School. World War II brought destruction as Manualas became a Japanese settlement, though guerrillas hid in the hills of Zou.

After the war, the town regained progress, with the San Miguel Brewery expanding and various industries like the Bonifacio Sawmill and Tamaraw Studio opening. By 1951, Manualas Elementary School was completed, and new businesses and homes continued to rise, making it a thriving community.
